Sh. Rattan Lal Kataria lauds passage of labor reform bills by Parliament, says for the first time since Independence, labor laws are going to be changed in the country

The Minister of State for Jal Shakti and Social Justice & Empowerment Sh. Rattan Lal Kataria today thanked Prime Minister Sh. Narendra Modi and Union Labour Minister Sh. Santosh Gangwar on passing of three labour law bills in Parliament. Sh. Rattan Lal Kataria, met Union Cabinet Minister Santosh Gangwar in the Lok Sabha and thanked the Government on the matter. Sh. Kataria said that it is for the first time in 73 years after Independence that labor laws are going to be changed. After passing of these bills, 50 crore workers will come under the ambit of social security. These laws will not only provide social security to the workers but will also bring revolutionary changes in business.

 

While conveying his thanks to the Prime Minister, Sh. Kataria said that the Constitution maker of India, Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar had voiced the interests of the workers and today PM Narendra Modi has fulfilled the dreams of Dr. Ambedkar ji. He further added that now both organized and unorganized workers will get various facilities after passing of these bills. In these bills, the central government has arranged social security fund which will benefit the workers of unorganized sector. It will now be mandatory to give appointment letters to all the workers.

 

Union Minister Rattan Lal Kataria said that Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi is taking the country towards a new direction, in which new public welfare laws are being made for all sections of the Society.
