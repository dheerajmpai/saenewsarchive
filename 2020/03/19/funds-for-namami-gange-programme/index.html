Funds for Namami Gange Programme

During the financial year 2019-20, the final allocation for the Namami Gange Programme is Rs.1,553.44 crore against the Budget Allocation of Rs.1,970.00 crore. The Government of India has released Rs.1,553.40 crore to National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) during the current financial year till 29 February 2020. The expenditure as on 13.03.2020 is Rs.2,453.91 crore which includes the carry forward funds of previous year.

The final allocation for Namami Gange Programme for the financial year 2018-19 was Rs.2,370.00 crore against which Government of India has released Rs.2,307.50 crore to NMCG. The expenditure was Rs.2,626.54 crore which includes the carry forward funds of previous year. The final allocation for financial year 2019-20 is sufficient to meet the funds requirement of current financial year.

The Budgetary Allocation for the Namami Gange Programme, for the financial year 2020-21 is Rs.1,640.02 crore. Based on the progress of projects under the programme, if additional funds are required, the revised allocation will be sought from the Government at RE stage.

This information was given by the Union Minister of State for Jal Shakti & Social Justice and Empowerment, Shri Rattan Lal Kataria in a written reply in Lok Sabha today.
