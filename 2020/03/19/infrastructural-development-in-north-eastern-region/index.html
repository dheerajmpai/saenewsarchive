Infrastructural Development in North Eastern Region

Union Minister of State (Independent Charge) Development of North-Eastern Region (DoNER), MoS PMO, Personnel, Public Grievances & Pensions, Atomic Energy and Space, Dr Jitendra Singh said  in a written reply to  question in  Rajya Sabha today that during 2014-19,  231 km of New Line have been commissioned in the North Eastern Region.  He said that    41 new Railway Stations were constructed on account of New Lines construction in North Eastern Region since 2014.

On the issue of roads, he said, Ministry of Road Transport and Highways has constructed 3178 km of roads in NER  during 2014-15 to 2018-19. During the same period, under Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), a total of 22,882  km of roads has been constructed,   while 987 Km roads has been constructed by Border Roads Organisation (BRO).

Ministry of DoNER under North East Road Sector Development Scheme (NERSDS) and North East Road Sector Development Scheme (NERSDS- EAP) has completed 187.13 km roads in 5 projects.

Out of 96 projects of road and bridges sector sanctioned by Ministry of DoNER under NLCPR/NESIDS/Special Development Packages of Assam in the North Eastern States during the period 01.04.2014 to till date, 90 projects costing Rs. 2174.70 crore are ongoing and 6 projects costing Rs. 139.90 crore have been completed. NEC has completed 33 road projects during 2014-15 to 2018-19.  Works for 20 roads projects at an estimated cost of Rs.1768 crore for 1075 Kms are ongoing.

Road construction in NER states is undertaken through schemes of Ministries/Departments which include SARDP-NE and Bharatmala Pariyojana of  Ministry of Road Transport and Highways; Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) scheme of Ministry of Rural Development; and under various schemes of  Ministry of DoNER  and NEC. Besides, roads are also constructed in NER by Border Roads Organisation (BRO) under Ministry of Defence.
