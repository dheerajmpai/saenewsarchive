Works under Jal Shakti

The Ministry of Jal Shakti has been created by the Government in May, 2019 to deal with water issues in an integrated manner under a single umbrella. The Ministry includes two Departments namely,Â  the Department of Water Resources, River Development & Ganga Rejuvenation (DoWR, RD & GR) and the Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation (DoDW & S).

Water Resources Projects are planned, funded, executed and maintained by the State Governments themselves as per their own resources and priority. To supplement their efforts and to encourage sustainable development and efficient management of water resources, the Central Government provides technical and financial assistance to State Governments through various schemes and programmes.

Out of 99 prioritized projects under Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) – Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP), 14 projects (and 7 phases) are in the State of Madhya Pradesh. During 2016-20 (so far) Central Assistance (CA) and State Share of Rs.588.86 crore and 824.43 crore respectively have been released for these projects. An additional Ultimate Irrigation Potential (UIP) of 162.19 Th.ha has been created during 2016-19. Out of above 14 projects (and 7 phases), 8 projects (and 03 phases) have been reported to be completed in Madhya Pradesh.

In Madhya Pradesh there are 14 projects in the list of 99 prioritized projects and all have been included under CADWM programme. These projects target CADWM works in balance Culturable Command Area (CCA) of 5.95 lakh ha. The total cost of these projects is estimated at Rs.2536.98 crore

with central share of Rs.1259.00 crore. During 2016-20 (till date), Central share of Rs.251.44 crore has been released to Madhya Pradesh for CADWM works of these projects, State has reported completion of CAD works in CCA of 2.23 lakh ha. has been reported by State during this period.
