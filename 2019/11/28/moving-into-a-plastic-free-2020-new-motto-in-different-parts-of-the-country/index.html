Moving into a plastic free 2020, new motto in different parts of the country.

Kerala has banned the manufacture, sale and storage of one-time use plastic items from January 1 2020. The prohibition was imposed on the basis of recommendation  of an expert committee appointed to suggest ways to reduce plastic waste. The exempted items are plastic for export purposes, plastic used in pharma industry, materials made of compostable or biodegradable plastics. 

Sever action is set to be taken against defaulters with fines starting from Rs.10,000 to closure and a fine of Rs. 50,000 under Environment Protection Act, 1986.

In a bid to reduce the use of plastic, a Hyderabad-based activist has started a campaign called ‘Bring Plastic, Take Sapling’ which aims at replacing plastics with greenery. Ram Dosapati, the activist is distributing plant saplings in exchange for plastic bags.

Addressing the citizens of the country during the 57th episode of ‘Mann ki Baat’, Prime Minister Narendra Modi said that 130 crore Indian have resolved to get rid of single-use plastic on the occasion of Mahatma Gandhi’s 150th birth anniversary.
