Centre for Silk Weaving

Government of India is implementing schemes in all places across India including Tamil Nadu to improve the technique of weaving. Under Hathkargha Samvardhan Sahayata (HSS) technological up-gradation items like loom, jacquard, dobby and accessories are provided to the handloom weavers. The weaving trainings are also imparted in and outside block level clusters under National Handloom Development Programme (NHDP) and Comprehensive Handloom Cluster Development Scheme (CHCDS) across the country. Further for capacity building in textile sector SAMARTH scheme is being implemented with the broad objective to skill the youth for gainful and sustainable employment in the textile sector.

 

The Ministry of Textiles provides financial assistance for registering the designs/products of Handloom under Geographical Indication (GI) Act, 1999. So far, 65 handloom products and 6 product logos are registered under the Act.

 

Central Government has no such plan to buy student uniforms directly from handloom weaver/societies. However, certain State Governments are engaging weavers/societies for uniforms of government school students.

 

This Ministry has engaged 23 e-commerce agencies including Amazon and Flipkart for promotion of e-marketing of handloom products.

 

This information was given by the Union Minister of Textiles, Smriti Zubin Irani, in written reply in the Lok Sabha today.
