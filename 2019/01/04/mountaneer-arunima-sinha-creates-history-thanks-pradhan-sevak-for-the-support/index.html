Arunima Sinha becomes world’s first woman amputee to climb highest peak of AntarcticaArunima Sinha was the first female amputee in the world to have conquered Mt Everest. Her aim was to climb all the continents’ highest peaks and hoist the national flag of India. She has already done six peaks by 2014: Everest in Asia, Kilimanjaro in Africa, Elbrus in Europe, Kosciuszko in Australia, Aconcagua in Argentina and Carstensz Pyramid (Puncak Jaya) in Indonesia[5].She completed her final summit of Mount Vinson in Antarctica on Jan 4, 2019. She was thrown out of a fast running train by goons in 2011. Due to which she had lost both of her legs.

She wrote the book “Born again on the mountain”, launched by Prime minister of India Narendra Modi in December 2014 explaining her story from the train accident to the summit to the Mount Everest.
