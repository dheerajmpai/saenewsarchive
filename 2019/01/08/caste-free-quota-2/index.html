MASSIVE : Quota for economically weaker passed in LS. TRS, TMC, Shiv Sena, LJP welcome the bill

The monumental bill to include Economically Weaker section (EWS) in the reservation system was passed in the Lok Sabha with humongous majority.

The BJP govt had proposed the bill on Jan 7th which provides reservation in education and employment in government institutions for individuals with income less than 8 Lakhs per annum.

Justice for everyone will be attained through this Bill… a major chunk of society will benefit from this BillThawar Chand Gehlot, Minister of Social Justice.

Several political parties including TMC, TRS and Shiv Sena welcomed the move saying the move would benefit the lower income housholds and work towards poverty elimination.

I welcome this Bill and thank the government that they decided on ‘Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas’ and took steps to implement it: Ram Vilas Paswan of Lok Janshakti Party (LJP) during discussion on quota bill.

List of legal hurdles long, yet it is a brave move by the government: Jithender Reddy of TRS.

Our party TRS… highly welcome every move to make the society inclusive and stronger. Therefore, we welcome this move of the government: AP Jithender Reddy of Telangana Rashtriya Samiti (TRS).

Congress opposed the move in the house. Finance Minister Arun Jaitley nailed congress by reading out the Congress manifesto which included reservation for the economically weaker section. He said

I am reminding Congress of its manifesto because its talk must match its walk.Arun Jaitley, Finance Minister of India

When the government has so many schemes for the economically weaker section, what is the need for reservation? This means that their schemes for the EWS have failed: M Thambi Durai of AIADMK.

LJP’s Ram Vilas Paswan went a step ahead and requested the government to consider reservation in the private sector as well.
