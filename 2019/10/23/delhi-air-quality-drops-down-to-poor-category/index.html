Delhi Air Quality drops down to ‘poor’ category

The Air Quality Index in Delhi has dropped back down to the ‘poor’ category as the wind direction has changed to northwesterly resulting in increasing smoke and dust particles in the air.

The people residing in Delhi are also complaining of a spike in respiratory problems and allergies. Many locals complain of feeling breathless and eye burns in the morning. SAFAR has advised ‘Sensitive Groups’ to reduce prolonged or heavy exertion. The locals are advised against strenuous activities and asthmatic stocks keep their medicines close.

According to a study by the Indian Council of Medical Research, about 4 lakh deaths in India in 2017 were due to air pollution which included 6.7 lakh deaths due to outdoor particulate matter.
