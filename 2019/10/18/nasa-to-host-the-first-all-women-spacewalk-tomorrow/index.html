NASA to host the first all-women spacewalk tomorrow

NASA astronauts Christina Koch and Jessica Meir will venture outside the International Space Station to replace faulty equipment on the stationâ€™s exterior in a first ever all-female spacewalk on 18 October 7:50 a.m. EDT.

It is the first spaceflight for both women who were selected in 2013 as a part of the first class of astronaut candidates of which 50% were women. Space travel is an industry that has struggled to understand and accommodate the needs of women. Even in this age the space radiation shields designed for women wrestles to fit the female body. Women have been performing spacewalks since 1984 and Meir will be the 15th woman to spacewalk.

Koch will be performing her fourth spacewalk as extravehicular crew member 1 (EV1) wearing a spacesuit with red stripes and Meir will be space walking for the first time as extravehicular crew member 2 (EV2) wearing a suit with no stripes. The Live coverage of this historical event will begin at 6:30 a.m. EDT.
