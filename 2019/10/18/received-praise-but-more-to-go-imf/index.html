Received praise but more to go: IMF

The international monetary fund has praised India for working on fundamentals for the development of its economy, it had also highlighted the major areas which should be addressed. it trimmed India’s growth forecast by 90 basis points and which is the second downward revision in seven months.

It should be noted that India was in a kind of economic slowdown a few weeks back and hasn’t completely recovered. some of the most important areas such as manufacturing, textiles, and automobile industries were facing economic crunch which caught the attention of the leaders over the issue.

The managing director of IMF, Kristalina Georgieva highlighted the need for long term drivers of growth which should be addressed for the more smooth functioning of the economy. she also suggested investment in human capital as a priority, also she highlighted the importance of injecting woman’s power in the labor force which could help India to excel further based on their talent.

The international economy plays a very important role in determining the status of any other national economy and like the rest of the world, India too is facing an economic slowdown.she also laid the importance of structural reforms which should be a priority for the Indian government. she also praised the step of consolidating banks which might resolve some of the major issues which the Indian economy is facing.
