Wildlife Photographer of the Year 2019 and the winning images announced

Chinese photographer Yongqing Bao has won the title of Wildlife Photographer of the year for 2019 by capturing a terrorised marmot in a humorous stance after he realised the fox behind him, that was all set to pounce.

Bao, director and chief ecological photographer of Qilian Mountains Nature Conservation Association of China, captured the Himalayan marmot that had just emerged from its burrow. The Tibetan fox with three hungry cubs to feed killed the marmot who was not long out of hibernation shortly after the photo, aptly titled The Moment, was taken.
The image won the overall title and in the Behaviour: Mammals category.14-year-old Cruz Edrmanntook of New Zealand is the winner of the Young Wildlife Photographer of the Year award for Night Glow, an underwater image of an iridescent bigfin reef squid, captured on a night dive off North Sulawesi, Indonesia before it disappeared into the darkness.

The contest also recognised photographs in 17 other categories. The Top 100 photos of 48,000 entries will be part of an exhibition that opens from 18 October in the Natural History Museum in London.
