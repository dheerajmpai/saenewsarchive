IMD issues orange alert in various districts of Kerala for two days

The Indian Meteorological department (IMD) has issued an Orange alert and has predicted heavy to very heavy rain accompanied with thunderstorm and lightening in the two days to come in the districts of Kerala.

According to the weather forecast agency, 12 districts come under the radar of the Orange alert today, including Thiruvananthapuram, Kollam, Alappuzha, Pathanamthitta, Kottayam, Idukki, Ernakulam, Thrissur, Palakkad, Malappuram, Kozhikode, and Wayanad.

An Orange alert for October 22 has also been issued for 13 districts, including Kollam, Alappuzha, Pathanamthitta, Kottayam, Idukki, Ernakulam, Thrissur, Palakkad, Malappuram, Kozhikode, Wayanad, Kannur and Kasargod.

The Orange alert by IMD requires the residents of the districts to “be prepared to face any situation arising out of severe weather condition”.

All government and private schools in Kochi are also closed due to heavy water-logging in many areas. Amid the weather condition, by-polls are also underway in five constituencies of the state- Vatiyoorkavu, Aroor, Konni, Ernakulam and Manjeswaram, the votes of which will be counted on October 24.
