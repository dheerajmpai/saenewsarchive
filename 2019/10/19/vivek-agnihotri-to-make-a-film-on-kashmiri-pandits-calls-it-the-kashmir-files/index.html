Vivek Agnihotri to make a film on Kashmiri Pandits, calls it ‘The Kashmir Files’

Vivek Agnihotri, the director of Chocolate, Buddha in a traffic jam, The Tashkent files, is directing a new film on the exodus of Kashmiri Pandits. The film is tentatively named as ‘The Kashmir Files’ based on a popular poll on twitter. The director is reaching out to the survivors of the exodus and is likely to contain real life incidents.

Vivek Agnihotri had directed several successful film. His last film, the Tashkent Files, made its record to be the longest running film in 2019. Earlier he had directed Chocolate and hate story. He also directed a socio-political movie ‘Buddha in a Traffic Jam’ exposing the plot of Intellectual terrorism. The film was censored. He also authored a best selling book titled ‘Urban Naxals’.

The Kashmiri Pandits faced seven waves of exodus. The first in 1300 and the seventh one in 1990s. Millions of Kashmiri Hindus have fled the valley since the Islamic rule in 1300. Yet these atrocities turn out be one of the least documented in the history.
